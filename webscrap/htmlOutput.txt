<p>Our graduates discover that <strong>computer science (CS)</strong> opens up a world of possibilities. </p>
<p>Computer scientists enjoy <strong>exceptional career opportunities</strong>, in settings ranging from large, established companies to adventurous new start-ups. They are also well qualified to pursue graduate study in a number of fields.</p>
<p>Our students are <strong>creative, analytical problem-solvers</strong>. This is a rich, collaborative and varied field that you will find challenging, no matter where your individual interests lie.</p>
<p>And there is more to CS than programming. While <strong>software engineering</strong> is an important skill, computer scientists also <strong>work with robots</strong> and other physical devices, <strong>design hardware that runs faster </strong>and more efficiently, and <strong>apply machine learning techniques</strong> to gain insight from large data sets—to name just a few examples.</p>
<p>Because CS has become highly <strong>interconnected with medicine, business and many other fields</strong>, it is a great fit with other interests you may have. You will enjoy a strong career outlook while having an <strong>impact on society</strong>.</p>

<a name="advisingandcareerstext"></a><h2>Advising</h2>
<p>The undergraduate coordinators in the Department of Computer Sciences are ready to help students with questions about the major, L&amp;S degree requirements and policy, and course selection. Information on academic advising for students interested or declared in the computer sciences major is posted to the <a href="https://www.cs.wisc.edu/undergraduate/undergraduate-advisors/" target="_blank">Computer Sciences advising page</a>. </p>
<h2>CAREERS</h2>
<p>Demand for those with a computer sciences education is exceptionally strong. According to figures from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the vast majority of growth in STEM (science, technology, engineering, and math) occupations through 2020 will occur within computing fields.</p>
<p>Computer sciences majors are encouraged to begin working on their career exploration and preparation soon after arriving on campus to explore different career paths, participate in co-ops or summer internships, prepare for the job search and/or graduate school applications, and network with professionals in the field.</p>
<p><strong>Department of Computer Sciences: </strong>the department hosts one major <a href="https://www.cs.wisc.edu/connect/job-fair" target="_blank">career fair</a> per year, in the fall, as well as other opportunities to connect with employers, such as technical talks and information sessions.</p>
<p><strong>SuccessWorks at the College of Letters &amp; Science:</strong> SuccessWorks offers two major career fairs per year, assists with resume writing and interviewing skills, and offers individual career advising appointments for L&amp;S students.</p>
<p><strong>Engineering Career Services (ECS):</strong> <a href="https://ecs.engr.wisc.edu/public/index.php" target="_blank">ECS</a> offers two major career fairs per year, assists with resume writing and interviewing skills, and hosts workshops on the job search. </p>
<h2>L&amp;S career resources</h2> <p>Every L&amp;S major opens a world of possibilities.  <a href="https://successworks.wisc.edu/" target="_blank">SuccessWorks</a> at the College of Letters &amp; Science helps students turn the academic skills learned in their major, certificates, and other coursework into fulfilling lives after graduation, whether that means jobs, public service, graduate school or other career pursuits.</p> <p>In addition to providing basic support like resume reviews and interview practice, SuccessWorks offers ways to explore interests and build career skills from their very first semester/term at UW all the way through graduation and beyond.</p> <p>Students can explore careers in one-on-one advising, try out different career paths, complete internships, prepare for the job search and/or graduate school applications, and connect with supportive alumni and even employers in the fields that inspire them.</p> <ul> <li><a
